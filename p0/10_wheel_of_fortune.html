Wheel of Fortune

Cycles, Destiny, and Divine Intervention

Our exploration of the Rider-Waite Tarot Deck brings us to the Wheel of Fortune, a symbol of cycles, destiny, and divine intervention. In this card, a wheel turns, and various figures, representing the four fixed signs of the zodiac—Leo, Taurus, Scorpio, and Aquarius—occupy each corner. A sphinx sits atop the wheel, symbolizing mystery, wisdom, and the enigmatic forces that govern the cycles of life.

The Wheel of Fortune represents the ever-turning wheel of fate, where individuals experience the highs and lows of life. The figures on the wheel symbolize the inevitable changes that come with the passage of time, and the sphinx reflects the wisdom required to navigate these cycles with grace and understanding.

The outer circle of the wheel is inscribed with the letters T, A, R, and O, which spell "TARO," suggesting a connection to the tarot itself and the cyclical nature of the human experience. The wheel turns in both directions, highlighting the cyclical nature of life's experiences and the perpetual movement of destiny.

In a tarot reading, the Wheel of Fortune often emerges as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life, the influence of fate, and the interconnectedness of all things. It signifies a time of change, both in external circumstances and internal growth, and encourages individuals to adapt to the shifting tides of destiny.

The Wheel of Fortune also emphasizes the role of divine intervention and the mysterious forces that guide the course of one's life. It suggests that some events are beyond personal control, and individuals are urged to surrender to the natural flow of destiny.

From a psychological perspective, the Wheel of Fortune represents the acceptance of life's ups and downs, embracing change, and understanding that personal growth often comes through experiencing a variety of circumstances. It encourages individuals to navigate the cycles with resilience and openness to the lessons each phase brings.

In a reversed position, the Wheel of Fortune may suggest resistance to change, a fear of the unknown, or a reluctance to accept the cyclical nature of life. It could indicate a need for surrendering control and embracing the inherent uncertainties of the journey. In such cases, the reversed Wheel of Fortune serves as a gentle reminder to trust the unfolding of destiny and find wisdom in the midst of change.

Archetypally, the Wheel of Fortune aligns with the archetype of destiny and the cosmic order that governs the cycles of life. It symbolizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the unfolding of events according to a higher plan.

Societally, the Wheel of Fortune encourages communities to acknowledge the impermanence of circumstances and embrace change with resilience. It emphasizes the importance of adapting to the cycles of life and finding meaning in the ever-turning wheel of destiny.

In conclusion, the Wheel of Fortune in the Rider-Waite Tarot Deck invites us to recognize the cyclical nature of life and embrace the mysteries of destiny. It encourages individuals to navigate change with openness, adaptability, and a willingness to surrender to the forces that guide the course of their journey.
